    • Project Magma 2025 Semester 1 - Communities Against Alcohol Harm



    Project Magma is a student-led clubat the University of Auckland that uses and develops the skills of Science and Engineering students to help not for profit organisations carry out their missions.

    In 2025, I was in a team of 6 students that worked with Communities Against Alcohol Harm (CAAH) to develop a tool that allowed them to automate the finding of new alcohol licence applications around New Zealand. This tool was made using python and AWS services, and it allows CAAH to keep on top of new applications without having to manually search for them.

    This project was a great introduction to working with a real-world client and allowed me to devlopment my skills in all stages of a project life cycle. I was chosen to be the team secretary, which meant I was responsible for taking minutes during client meetings and communicating with the client on behalf of the team.
